Justin Harmon interviewed Kayson L Conlin to gather information for their research, focusing on Kayson L Conlin's career journey, job hunt experiences, and tools used for resume management. Kayson L Conlin shared their positive experiences with Teal, an AI-powered service that tailors resumes to job requirements and helps with cover letters, highlighting its effectiveness in boosting confidence and generating more attention from employers. Job Hunt Pain Points and Resume Tools Justin Harmon inquired about Kayson L Conlin's job hunt experiences, specifically focusing on resume-related pain points and the tools they used. Kayson L Conlin initially used a single resume that they would tweak, but they discovered that their resume wasn't well-written for initial company representation (00:09:46). They then found a service called Teal, which uses AI to tailor resumes to job requirements by selecting and customizing bullet points and helping write cover letters (00:11:17).
Teal Features and Effectiveness Kayson L Conlin described Teal as a web app with a desktop app and Chrome extension that reads job postings, saves information to a job database, and assesses resume fit (00:12:59). They explained that Teal aims for a 65-75% correlation between the job requirements and the resume to appear authentic yet optimized (00:14:08). Kayson L Conlin noted that Teal helped boost their confidence and garnered more attention and follow-ups during their job search (00:11:17).
Resume Management in Teal Kayson L Conlin elaborated on how Teal manages resumes, explaining that users create a master copy with numerous bullet points for each job, and Teal then suggests which bullet points to include based on a job description to avoid overly long resumes. They also mentioned that Teal uses AI to generate bullet points and cover letters, which is more accurate than general chatbots like Claude or ChatGPT because it's based on the user's specific resume information (00:15:17). Kayson L Conlin compared general AI to an "eager intern" that prioritizes impressing over accuracy (00:16:33).
Teal's Best and Missing Features Justin Harmon asked Kayson L Conlin to identify the best and most desired missing features of Teal. Kayson L Conlin praised Teal's ease of use, particularly its ability to parse and divide an existing design-focused resume into bullet points, despite previous challenges with visual resumes (00:17:34). They also appreciated the minimal design options but wished for more personalization in templates (00:18:44). Kayson L Conlin noted that a missing feature is the ability to pull information from a wider range of websites, as it seems geared more towards main job sites, leading to manual input for some applications (00:21:07).
Content and Role Management in Prototype Kayson L Conlin provided feedback on a prototype, noting that their expectation for an "add content" feature would be to include multimedia like portfolios or presentations, rather than just text editing (00:30:12). They also highlighted the importance of being able to add and differentiate multiple positions or roles within a single company, which is common for long-term employment (00:31:33). Kayson L Conlin then explained that Teal addresses this by allowing individual manipulation of company and position details through a parent-child relationship, enabling the addition of separate fields for multiple positions under a single company (00:33:04).
Resume Versioning and Success Tracking in Teal Kayson L Conlin explained that Teal saves every version of a created resume and allows users to build off existing versions or create new ones from a base resume. When a new version is created, it can retroactively add content to previous resumes, and Teal automatically titles new resumes based on the company and position title from job postings (00:36:04). Kayson L Conlin further noted that Teal offers the option to populate new resumes based on previous ones or tailor them to a job description, helping users identify which bullet points are most effective by tracking interview processes and job offers (00:38:39).
Teal vs. Prototype Functionality and Future Desires Justin Harmon asked Kayson L Conlin to compare the prototype's potential to Teal, considering one top feature from Teal. Kayson L Conlin observed that the prototype focuses on centralizing information into one place, whereas Teal excels at disseminating that information based on specific job requirements. They suggested that if the prototype could expand its current feature of pulling information from the internet to also auto-populate job applications, similar to Teal's functionality, it would be highly useful (00:40:10). Kayson L Conlin emphasized the value of accurate field alignment for correct prepopulation, as they often use Teal to generate PDFs that auto-populate job applications (00:41:38).
